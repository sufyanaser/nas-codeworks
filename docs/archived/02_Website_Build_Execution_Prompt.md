# NAS CodeWorks — Website Build Execution Prompt

You are working on the NAS CodeWorks website.

Your task is to build the first version of the public website.

Do not redesign the strategy.
Do not rewrite the approved copy.
Do not change the positioning.
Do not add services.
Do not create a SaaS-style site.
Do not create a software-company portfolio.
Do not create a dashboard/product landing page.

Build the website according to the approved strategy, copy, navigation, CTA, visual identity, and the latest Identity Layer Update.

# Project Context

NAS CodeWorks is an Iraqi operational problem-solving studio.

It helps Iraqi small and medium businesses reduce operational chaos, manual work, scattered information, delayed reports, and unclear daily workflows through custom internal tools with clear Arabic interfaces.

NAS CodeWorks is not positioned as:

* a generic software company
* a SaaS company
* a digital agency
* a freelancer portfolio
* a cloud ERP competitor
* a warehouse-only software company
* a NAS FM radio/media extension

The website must make the visitor feel:

“This team understands the operational mess inside my company.”

Not:

“This is a company that builds apps.”

# Identity Layer Update — Mandatory

A new identity layer has been approved and must override any earlier visual decision if there is conflict.

The core visual transformation is now:

Fragmented Signals
→ Operational Center
→ Relationship Mapping
→ Structured Operations
→ Operational Clarity

The previous shorthand (Scattered Information → Operational Center → Structured Operations → Operational Clarity) remains valid as a condensed summary. The expanded version above is the governing model for all build decisions.

## Governing Rule: The Center Matters More Than The Elements

This rule overrides any design decision that places the fragments above the center.

The scattered elements — Excel, WhatsApp, files, reports, employees — are secondary signals.

They are examples of fragmentation. They are not the identity of NAS CodeWorks.

The main idea is the absence of a center that sees the relationship between these parts.

Therefore:

* The Operational Center must be the strongest visual anchor in every diagram, hero, and graphic.
* Excel, WhatsApp, files, and reports appear as secondary fragments — lighter, smaller, flowing toward the center.
* Relationship paths between fragments and center must be visible — not just proximity.
* The center must suggest understanding, not just collection.

If the visitor remembers “this company fixes Excel/WhatsApp problems,” the visual has failed.

If the visitor remembers “this company helps me see the full picture of my business,” the visual has succeeded.

## The Missing Center

The website must express the deeper idea:

Information already exists inside the company, but no one stands at the center of it.

In many companies:

* the employee knows one part of the picture
* the manager knows another part
* Excel carries another part
* WhatsApp carries another part
* files carry another part
* reports carry another part

The problem is not lack of information.

The problem is the absence of a center that sees the relationship between all these parts simultaneously.

NAS CodeWorks represents that operational center — and reveals the relationships between the parts.

The primary visual subject is the Operational Center — not the scattered elements.

The hero visual must show the Operational Center as the strongest visual anchor: largest, clearest, most visually weighted element.

## Fragments are Secondary

The scattered fragments — file, report, message, follow-up, employee node, paper, data — appear as:

* small geometric shapes or simple labels
* lighter opacity than the center
* positioned as inputs, not as main subjects
* connected to the center by visible relationship paths

These fragments are signals of fragmentation, not the identity of NAS CodeWorks.

## Relationship Paths are Required

The hero must show visible paths connecting fragments to the center.

These paths communicate: the center sees the relationship between the parts.

Not just: the parts are moving toward the center.

## Visual hierarchy

1. Operational Center — strongest visual anchor
2. Relationship paths — secondary weight
3. Fragments — lightest weight

## The center represents

Operational Center

A point of visibility that reveals the relationships between scattered operational information — not a software dashboard, not a product screen.

The section must communicate:

* The data exists. The files exist. The reports exist.
* Every employee knows their part.
* But no one sees the relationship between all parts at once.
* There is no center of operational visibility.
* This is the real problem — not Excel, not WhatsApp, not paper.

This section reframes the problem from “tool overload” to “missing center.”

## After first 10–15 seconds, the visitor must feel

“The problem is not that my company lacks software.”

“The problem is that what happens inside the work has no center — no one sees the full picture.”

“This team can help me see the full picture.”

## The center, not the fragments

The narrative must not dwell on Excel/WhatsApp/files as the main subject.

These appear once as examples, then the narrative moves immediately to the missing center.

The emotional hook is: “No one sees the full picture.” Not: “You use Excel too much.”

# Operational Center Hero Visual Requirements

Create a lightweight visual using CSS/SVG/HTML.

## Visual hierarchy — strictly enforced

1. **Center element** — the largest, clearest, most visually dominant element. Labeled: `مركز الرؤية التشغيلية`. Color: Muted Teal. Visually stable and grounded.

2. **Relationship paths** — lines or arcs connecting fragments to the center. Show the center is receiving and understanding inputs, not just collecting them.

3. **Fragment nodes** — small, lightweight geometric shapes with Arabic labels. Smaller than the center. Lighter opacity.

## Fragment labels (secondary, not dominant)

* ملف
* تقرير
* رسالة
* متابعة
* موظف
* ورق
* أرقام

Do not use actual software icons. Do not use Excel logo, WhatsApp icon, or any real brand mark. Use abstract geometric shapes + Arabic text label only.

## What the visual must communicate

Fragmented Signals → Operational Center → Relationship Mapping → Operational Clarity

Not: "here are your tools." But: "here is the center that sees all of them together."

## What it must not look like

* a software dashboard
* an app screenshot
* a collection of app icons
* fragments that appear more prominent than the center

## Motion (optional, restrained only)

If motion is used:

* fragments drift slowly toward center — subtle, not dramatic
* relationship paths draw in gradually
* center appears last or pulses once to establish dominance
* `prefers-reduced-motion`: show static version, center already formed, paths already visible
