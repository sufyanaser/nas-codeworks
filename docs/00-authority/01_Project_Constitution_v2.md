## 1. What NAS CodeWorks is

NAS CodeWorks is an **Iraqi operational problem-solving studio**.

It helps Iraqi small and medium businesses reduce operational chaos, manual work, scattered information, delayed reports, and unclear daily workflows through custom internal tools with clear Arabic interfaces.

It does not sell software as the main idea. **Software is the delivery mechanism.**

## 2. What NAS CodeWorks is not

- a generic software company
- a SaaS company
- a digital agency
- a freelancer portfolio
- a cloud ERP / CRM competitor
- a warehouse-only software company
- a NAS FM radio/media extension

## 3. Core positioning

Positioning hierarchy (unchanged, authoritative):

```
Operational Problem-Solving Studio
→ Custom Tool Builder
→ Software as delivery mechanism
```

Key operating belief: **Do not start from the application. Start from the problem.**

The website must make the visitor feel:

> "This team understands the operational mess inside my company."

Not:

> "This is a company that builds apps."

## 4. Operational Center philosophy (governing)

### 4.1 The Missing Center

The problem in small and medium businesses is **not lack of information**. The information already exists — but it is scattered between people, Excel, WhatsApp, paper, files, and reports.

The real problem is the **absence of a center** that sees the relationship between all these parts at once.

NAS CodeWorks helps institutions **restore a center of operational visibility** inside the work, and reveals the relationships between the scattered parts.

### 4.2 The Center matters more than the elements (governing rule)

The scattered elements — Excel, WhatsApp, files, reports, employees, paper — are **secondary signals of fragmentation**, not the identity of NAS CodeWorks.

- The Operational Center must be the **strongest anchor** in every diagram, hero, screen, and graphic.
- Fragments appear lighter, smaller, and positioned as inputs flowing toward the center.
- **Relationship paths** between fragments and center must be visible — not just proximity.
- The center must suggest **understanding**, not just collection.

Test: if the visitor remembers *"this company fixes Excel/WhatsApp problems,"* the work has failed. If the visitor remembers *"this company helps me see the full picture of my business,"* it has succeeded.

### 4.3 Core transformation model

Governing (expanded) model:

```
Fragmented Signals
→ Operational Center
→ Relationship Mapping
→ Structured Operations
→ Operational Clarity
```

Valid condensed summary:

```
Scattered Information → Operational Center → Structured Operations → Operational Clarity
```

### 4.4 The NAS circle

NAS CodeWorks inherits the NAS circle as a **strategic philosophy only** — bringing closer the distance between what actually happens inside the work and what management can see and understand.

It does **not** inherit the circle as a radio/media graphic. Rejected: media circles, broadcast waves, radio rings, sound/frequency symbols. If a circle is used, it must be incomplete, formed from units, emerging from organized elements — the circle is the **result** of organization, not a container of fragments.

## 9. Content governance (PRESERVED)

### Mandatory message order

```
Problem → Impact → Understanding → Suitable Path → Outcome → Trust → Problem-Description CTA
```

Do not reverse this order. Every page/screen starts from a problem or operational tension — never from a service list, technology, screenshots, dashboards, pricing, or features.

### Language

Use: simple professional Arabic, light Iraqi-white tone, business-owner language, short clear sentences, practical examples, daily-work vocabulary.
Avoid: heavy formal Arabic, bureaucratic language, technical jargon, startup hype, generic digital-transformation phrases, software-company language.

### Technology communication

Technology may be mentioned only to explain implementation/delivery **after trust is built**. Technology is never the hero message.

### Case Studies philosophy (PRESERVED)

Case studies are **not portfolio pieces**. They are problem-recognition stories. Each follows:

```
Problem → Previous state → Impact → Solution path → Outcome
```

No project grid, screenshot gallery, "our work", portfolio cards, or interface showcase. Public label: `قصص المشاكل`.

### Contact page logic (PRESERVED)

The contact page is a **Problem Discovery Entry Point** — never "Request a quote", "Book consultation", "Request software", or "Project inquiry". It collects a description of the operational problem (see Section 11.8, Guided Diagnostic Flow). No budget question in the initial form; no app type, platform, page count, features, technology, database, or integrations.

## 10. Visual identity (PRESERVED)

- **Core concept:** Operational Clarity. **Supporting concept:** Structured Operations. **Updated layer:** Operational Center.
- **Brand personality:** Professional · Practical · Clear · Approachable · Human. Never cold, bureaucratic, or corporate-heavy.
- **Logo:** Wordmark + Geometric Operational Mark (see NAS Core Symbol, 11.10). Forbidden: code brackets, radio/sound waves, microphone, warehouse icon as main mark, dashboard icon, SaaS abstract mark, N-only logo without operational meaning.

### Color

| Role | Direction | HEX |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Deep Graphite / Ink Navy | `#17212B`, `#1E2A36` |
| Secondary | Muted Teal / Operational Blue-Green | `#2E7D7A`, `#3A8F8A` |
| Background | Warm Off-White / Soft Operational Gray | `#F6F4EF`, `#F2F4F3` |
| Text | Near Black / Slate | `#1A1D21`, `#27313A` |
| Accent | Restrained Amber (limited use only) | `#C98A2E`, `#D49A3A` |

Avoid neon, heavy/startup gradients, generic SaaS blue, loud agency colors, radio/media color language.

### Typography

Arabic-first modern sans-serif. Reference: **IBM Plex Sans Arabic** (fallback: Noto Sans Arabic). Regular / Medium / Semibold / limited Bold. Avoid decorative Arabic fonts, heavy Kufi, thin low-readability fonts, overly technical fonts, too many families.

### Imagery / motifs

Abstract Operational Graphics + Structured Diagrams (not real photography). Allowed motifs: scattered points becoming structure, organized paths, before/after, status cards, connection lines, information layers, simplified tables, workflow maps. Forbidden: programmer/stock/team photos, laptops, hands on keyboards, generic dashboards, warehouse-only visuals, radio/media visuals, cartoon SaaS illustrations.

### Relationship with NAS FM

Inherits: NAS name, local trust, seriousness, geographic/local presence.
Does not inherit: radio waves, audio symbols, broadcast language, confusing media colors, NAS FM technical-department impression.

## 11. Experience Architecture — latest approved direction (governing)

This section consolidates the **latest approved project direction**. These decisions **supersede** any earlier framing of the website as a long-scrolling, single-renderer "multi-page marketing website." They preserve all positioning, services, pricing, CTA, contact logic, case-studies philosophy, and visual identity above.

### 11.1 Interactive Executive Presentation

The website is delivered as an **interactive executive presentation** addressed to a decision-maker (owner / manager) — not a static brochure or a passive scrolling page. It walks the visitor from operational problem → missing center → operational clarity as a deliberate, guided experience.

### 11.2 Operational Center Experience

The Operational Center is not only a hero graphic; it is the **organizing experience** of the entire site. Every screen reinforces the transformation `Fragmented Signals → Operational Center → Operational Clarity`, with the center as the strongest anchor on every screen (per Section 4.2).

### 11.3 Screen Engine

Content is presented as **discrete, intentional screens/states** advanced by user action, governed by a Screen Engine — not as one continuous document. Each screen carries one clear idea in the mandatory message order (Section 9).

### 11.4 No Global Scroll

The experience does **not** rely on a long global vertical scroll. Each screen is self-contained; movement between screens is deliberate (via the Hybrid Navigator), not free scrolling. Local scrolling **inside** a screen is permitted only where genuinely needed (e.g. long forms / overflow), and never as the primary navigation model.

### 11.5 Desktop Renderer

A **dedicated desktop presentation** of the Screen Engine, optimized for large screens — spatial layout of the Operational Center, executive-presentation feel, generous space, calm structure.

### 11.6 Mobile Renderer

A **dedicated mobile presentation** of the Screen Engine — not a mere responsive reflow of the desktop layout. It preserves the no-global-scroll, screen-based experience on small screens with mobile-appropriate interaction.

### 11.7 Hybrid Navigator

The navigation model **combines top-level navigation with screen-to-screen progression**. Visitors can both jump to an approved section (Section 8) and move through the guided flow. The final nav item (`ابدأ بوصف المشكلة`) acts as the primary CTA. The navigator must not become a SaaS mega-menu and must not expose Products/Pricing/Packages.

### 11.8 Guided Diagnostic Flow

The Contact / Problem Discovery experience is delivered as a **guided diagnostic** that walks the owner step by step through describing the operational problem. It consolidates — and does not change — the approved Problem Discovery logic:

1. الاسم الكامل · 2. اسم الشركة أو المؤسسة · 3. المدينة · 4. رقم الهاتف أو WhatsApp · 5. البريد الإلكتروني إن وجد
6. نوع المشكلة تقريباً (أداة داخلية / أرشفة وتنظيم / تقارير ومتابعة / غير متأكد)
7. ما المشكلة التي تريد حلها؟ · 8. كيف تتم إدارة هذا العمل حالياً؟ · 9. من يستخدم هذه العملية؟ · 10. ما أثر المشكلة؟ · 11. أمثلة / مرفقات اختيارية · 12. التوقيت المتوقع

Submit: `إرسال وصف المشكلة`. Success: `تم استلام وصف المشكلة، وسنراجعه لفهم الوضع الحالي.` No budget, no app type, no technology questions.

### 11.9 Layered Interface

The UI is built in **layers** (base screen → Operational Center layer → fragment & relationship layers → overlays/panels) rather than stacked scrolling sections. Layering enforces the center-over-fragments hierarchy (Section 4.2): the center sits above, fragments and relationship paths render as lighter supporting layers.

### 11.10 NAS Core Symbol

The **NAS Core Symbol** is the formalized central brand symbol representing the Operational Center: an incomplete circle / center that emerges from organized fragments and their relationship paths. It is the visual heart of the Operational Center Experience and the Desktop/Mobile Renderers. It inherits NAS philosophy as a strategic idea only — never radio, broadcast, sound-wave, frequency, or microphone forms (Section 4.4). The center must read as *understanding*, not *containment*.

## 15. Governing question

Every strategy, content, identity, UX, and build decision must answer:

> Does this help the visitor understand that their company's scattered information can become a clear **Operational Center** — delivered as a guided, screen-based **Interactive Executive Presentation** — and lead them to **describe their current operational problem**?
